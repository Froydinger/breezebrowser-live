How does Breeze AI work?

Press ⌘E to open it. It runs Qwen2.5 entirely on your Mac — nothing you ask ever leaves your device. Ask it to "summarize this page," look something up on the web, or set a reminder, and it decides which tool to use on its own.

Use the AI on selected text

Open the assistant (⌘E), then highlight any text on the page. It appears as a chip above the input and becomes the focus of your next question. Hit the magnifier on the chip to web-search that exact text instead.

Search the web

Just ask — when a question needs live info, Breeze AI opens your default search engine in a tab, reads the results, and answers with sources. No API key needed.

Set a reminder

Just tell the assistant: "remind me in 20 minutes to call mom." Breeze fires a native notification when it's time, and the reminder shows up under the Reminders tab where you can cancel it. (Notifications must be allowed — see Privacy above.)

Pin sites & make tab groups

Right-click any tab → Pin Tab to keep it as an app icon at the top of the sidebar (drag to reorder; size in Appearance). Right-click → Move to Group to file tabs into a named, collapsible group. Pins and groups persist even after you close the tab.

Shortcuts cheat-sheet

⌘T new tab · ⌘W close · ⌘S toggle sidebar · ⌘E AI · ⌘L address bar · ⌘D bookmark · ⌘Y history · ⇧⌘J downloads · ⌘⇧N incognito · ⌥⌘P picture-in-picture · ⇧⌘D dark mode.

A site looks broken

Right-click the page → Fix This Site (Clear Data & Reload), or use the trash icon next to the bookmark star to clear that site's cache. Breeze blocks ads at the network level only, so it never hides real page content.

Privacy & your data

History and saved passwords are encrypted on this device with your system keychain. Incognito tabs (⌘⇧N) keep nothing and wipe on close. The AI is fully local. Nothing syncs anywhere.

Passkey support with iCloud Keychain requires a code-signed build; USB security keys work everywhere.
